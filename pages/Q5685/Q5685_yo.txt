Anton Pavlovich Chekhov (Rọ́síà: Анто́н Па́влович Че́хов, pípè [ɐnˈton ˈpavləvʲɪtɕ ˈtɕexəf]; 29 January 1860 – 15 July 1904) was a Russian physician, dramatist and author who is considered to be among the greatest writers of short stories in history. His career as a dramatist produced four classics and his best short stories are held in high esteem by writers and critics.  Chekhov practised  as a doctor throughout most of his literary career: "Medicine is my lawful wife", he once said, "and literature is my mistress."
