Gaius Caesar oþre naman Iulius (101 – 15 Hrēþmōnaþ 44 BCE) ƿæs Rōmānisc heretoga and rīca se man hygþ sƿa ān Rōmāniscan stǣre mǣst getæsendum mannum.
Caesar ƿæs gearcyning þæs Rōmaāniscan Cyneƿīsan and for seofen ƿintrum ƿyrhtedon Gaius Iulius Caesar, Marcus Licinius Crassus and Gnaeus Pompeius Magnus hiere ƿeard ofer þǣm rice ætgædre oð Crasses dēaðe in 53 beforan Criste.
Caesar ƿeard ealdorman oððe proconsul ofer Profense and mid his legium gehiersumede eall Gallie. Syxtigum ƿintrum ǣr ðam ðe Crist ƿære acenned, Gaius Iulius, Rōmāna kāsere, mid hund-eahtatigum scipum, gesōhte Brytene ac æfter his herigunge beebode he on Gallie.  His endmesta sige þæs Galliscan geƿinn ƿæs æt Alesie þǣm beorge.
Æfter his sige on Galƿallum, fōr Caesar ofer Rubicone þǣre ēa on Eotolƿare and sōhtede þæt rice ofer Rōme and his landrīce, and in þǣm ingeƿinn þæræfter frummede he sige and ƿeard tictator.
Man him ofslōg in þǣm Ƿitenahūs on Ides Martii, 44 beforan Criste.
Nemnaþ men ðæne mōnaþ Mǣdmōnaþ gehƿǣr Iulius.
